Bridgerton

"Bridgerton" is a popular Netflix period drama series set in Regency-era London during the early 19th century. Produced by Shonda Rhimes and based on Julia Quinn's novels, the show revolves around the lives of the aristocratic Bridgerton family and the high society of the Ton. The story begins with the debutante season, where young ladies are presented to society to find suitable husbands. In this world of opulence, grand parties, and matchmaking, there is a mysterious gossip columnist known as Lady Whistledown, whose identity remains a secret. Lady Whistledown's provocative and scandalous writings create drama and intrigue among the upper echelons of society, exposing secrets and affecting reputations. As the season progresses, secrets, lies, and unexpected alliances are unveiled, making "Bridgerton" a delightful blend of romance, drama, and scandal. The series explores themes of love, family, societal expectations, and the quest for identity in a glittering yet ruthless world.

Fun Facts

  1. Julie Andrews is the narrator.
  2. Queen Charlotte wasn't in the novels at all.
  3. Season 1 featured 5,000 costumes.
  4. Golda Rosheuvel (Queen Charlotte) originally auditioned for Lady Danbury.
  5. The Bridgerton's home was inspired by Princess Diana's Althorp Estate.
  6. One English estate was used for four different settings.
  7. Many of the exterior shots are CGI.
  8. There are Easter eggs just for fans of the books.
  9. Producers worked with historians on casting decisions.
  10. Queen Charlotte really did love Pomeranians.
